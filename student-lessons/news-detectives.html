Lesson 1: News Detectives üïµ

Goal: Teach students to tell facts from opinions, spot bias, and check news accuracy.

Duration: 70 minutes

Age Groups:

  • Middle School (11-14): Start with local news, basic fact-checking, and lots of examples.
  • High School (14-18): Dive into global news, analyze headlines, and discuss personalized news feeds.

Materials Needed:

  • News articles/videos from varied sources
  • Internet and note-taking tools

Lesson Steps:

  1. Kick-off (2 mins): Show a sensational headline (e.g., "Chocolate Cures All Diseases!"). Ask, "Is this true? How can we check?"
  2. Key Terms (3 mins):
    • Fact: Proven true (e.g., "Earth orbits the Sun").
    • Opinion: Personal belief (e.g., "Pizza is the best food").
    • Bias: Personal views affecting news presentation.
    • Source: Where info comes from.
  3. Take It In (15 mins): Watch video clips from two sources on the same topic. Note facts vs. opinions. Use a quiz tool to test what they've learned.
  4. Dig Deeper (25 mins): Read two articles on the same topic. Identify facts, opinions, and bias. Use online tools like LLMs to verify information but stress cross-referencing with reliable sources.
  5. Share & Compare (15 mins): In small groups, discuss:
    • One verifiable fact from the articles.
    • An example of opinion.
    • Any bias in the reporting.
  6. Think About It (10 mins): Discuss why news sources vary. Highlight the importance of multiple perspectives.

Keep Learning (optional):

Set up a regular "news detective" session where students bring in news to analyze.

Teacher Tips:

  • Stay neutral, promote seeing all sides.
  • Teach finding trustworthy sources.
  • Encourage questioning information while being open to various perspectives.
